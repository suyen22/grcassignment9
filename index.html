History of Cupcakes

Cupcakes trace their origins to the late 18th and early 19th centuries, when bakers in the United States began making small, individual cakes that could be baked more quickly than full-sized ones. The first known reference appears in American Cookery (1796), which included recipes for “light cakes” baked in small cups. By the 19th century, the term “cupcake” became popular, either because the cakes were baked in cups or because ingredients were measured by the cup. Their convenience, quick baking time, and individual portions made them especially popular in home kitchens, and over time they evolved into the decorated, frosted treats widely enjoyed today.
